Welcome

Oceans are the lifeblood of Earth, covering over 70% of the surface and hosting an incredible diversity of life. This site explores marine life, threats it faces, and how we can protect it.

Identification of Sealife

Sea life refers to all living things that live in the sea including:

  • Microorganisms: Phytoplankton, marine bacteria
  • Invertebrates: Jellyfish, octopus, coral
  • Vertebrates: Fish, marine reptiles (turtles), marine mammals (whales, dolphins)
  • Seabirds: Seagull, albatross

Methods to Study Sealife

  • Diving Surveys: Divers observe marine life directly in reefs and shallow waters
  • ROVs & Drones: Remotely operated vehicles explore deep seas
  • Tagging & Tracking: Animals are tagged with GPS to track migration
  • Underwater Cameras: Monitor marine behavior over time
  • DNA Sampling: Identifies species and genetic biodiversity

Ocean Zones

  • Sunlight Zone (Epipelagic): Top layer with most marine life & photosynthesis
  • Twilight Zone (Mesopelagic): Dimly lit, strange fish and squids
  • Midnight Zone (Bathypelagic): Pitch dark, bioluminescent creatures
  • Abyss & Trenches: Coldest, deepest zones with extreme pressure

Marine Ecosystems

  • Coral Reefs: Colorful, diverse habitats under threat from warming seas
  • Mangroves: Coastal forests that filter water and shelter juvenile fish
  • Deep Sea: Home to alien-like creatures and unknown biodiversity
  • Open Ocean: Largest habitat with whales, sharks, and plankton

Marine Food Chain

  • Producers: Phytoplankton, algae (base of the chain)
  • Primary Consumers: Zooplankton, small fish
  • Secondary Consumers: Jellyfish, bigger fish
  • Tertiary Consumers: Sharks, whales, dolphins
  • Decomposers: Bacteria that recycle nutrients

Threats to Marine Life

  • Plastic Waste: Entangles marine animals, microplastics enter the food chain
  • Climate Change: Ocean warming and acidification bleach reefs and alter ecosystems
  • Oil & Chemical Spills: Poison water and destroy habitats
  • Illegal Fishing: Includes overfishing, bottom trawling, and dynamite fishing

Conservation Strategies

  • Marine Protected Areas (MPAs)
  • Enforcing sustainable fishing laws
  • Promoting eco-friendly tourism
  • Reducing carbon and plastic footprints
